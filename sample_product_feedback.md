# Sample Product Feedback

## Purpose

This note shows how I would evaluate a stock research platform not just as a user, but as a power user and feedback partner.

The goal is to provide feedback that is clear, structured, and actionable.

---

## What Worked Well

### 1. Clear prioritization of stocks
The dashboard makes it easy to see which names deserve attention first.

Why this is useful:
An analyst should not have to scan the entire market manually when the strongest names can be surfaced automatically.

### 2. Sector-first framing is strong
Showing the best sector along with sector leaders adds useful market context.

Why this is useful:
Stocks usually perform better when sector momentum supports them.

### 3. Trade-ready table is practical
Entry, stop loss, target, risk %, and RS rating make the output more actionable.

Why this is useful:
This moves the platform beyond a watchlist and toward decision support.

### 4. Simple UI supports fast workflow
The layout is easy to read and works well for quick scanning during market hours.

---

## Gaps Observed

### 1. Fundamental layer is still light
The current system is stronger on technical and live screening signals than on deep fundamental validation.

Improvement suggestion:
Add company-level metrics such as ROE, ROCE, EPS growth, revenue growth, debt to equity, and valuation filters.

### 2. Explanation of score logic can be clearer
A user can see rankings, but may not immediately know why one stock ranks above another.

Improvement suggestion:
Add a "Why this stock ranked here" panel showing top contributing factors.

### 3. Sector score transparency can improve
Best sector is visible, but the scoring logic is not fully transparent to the end user.

Improvement suggestion:
Add a breakdown such as:
- number of strong stocks
- number of trade-ready names
- average RS rating
- average score

### 4. Benchmarking support can improve
A research platform should make it easy to compare output against manual validation.

Improvement suggestion:
Add a built-in benchmark workflow where the analyst can mark:
- valid output
- weak output
- false positive
- needs deeper review

---

## Suggested Features

### 1. Stock explanation card
For each stock, show:
- relative strength driver
- volume confirmation
- sector tailwind
- technical structure
- risk summary

### 2. Fundamental quick view
Add a small company snapshot:
- market cap
- EPS growth
- ROE
- debt/equity
- revenue trend
- valuation ratio

### 3. Research note export
Allow the analyst to turn a screened stock into a structured research note.

### 4. Analyst feedback loop
Add a feedback module where the analyst can tag outputs as:
- useful
- noisy
- incomplete
- high quality
- needs correction

This would be valuable for model and product improvement.

---

## Testing Mindset

If I were evaluating this platform daily, I would test it on:

- strong trending days
- weak market days
- choppy sessions
- sector rotation days
- earnings-driven moves
- false breakout conditions

This would help measure:
- ranking quality
- stability of output
- usability of the dashboard
- real research value

---

## Final Assessment

The dashboard is strong as a live screening and analyst workflow tool.

It already solves the problem of reducing manual market scanning and surfacing stronger names quickly.

The next level of improvement is to make it:
- more explainable
- more benchmarkable
- more fundamentally aware
- more useful for structured research validation
