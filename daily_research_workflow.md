# Daily Research Workflow

## Purpose

This document shows how I would use a stock research platform like this in a real daily workflow as a stock market research analyst.

The goal is to demonstrate that I can use the platform not only as a viewer, but as a practical power user.

---

## Workflow Objective

A strong research workflow should help me:

- reduce manual scanning
- identify strong sectors early
- shortlist high-quality stocks
- validate tool output against manual checks
- convert shortlisted names into research notes
- give useful feedback on platform quality

---

## Pre-Market Workflow

### 1. Review broader market context
Before using the dashboard, I would quickly check:
- index trend
- overnight global cues
- sector news
- earnings calendar
- any major event-driven names

### 2. Prepare research focus
I would define:
- which sectors may need attention
- which stocks are on watchlist
- whether the day may be trend-driven or event-driven

### 3. Open platform for first screen
At this stage, I want the platform to help me:
- detect strong sectors quickly
- identify any early leaders
- avoid wasting time on low-quality names

---

## During Market Hours

### Step 1: Check top-ranked stocks
I would first review:
- 1st Best Stock
- 2nd Best Stock
- Trade Ready section

Why:
This gives a fast snapshot of where strength is appearing.

### Step 2: Check best sector and sector leaders
I would then review:
- best sector card
- sector leader section
- whether multiple names in the same sector are appearing

Why:
Strong sector participation increases confidence in stock-level moves.

### Step 3: Validate technical quality
For shortlisted names, I would manually validate:
- price structure
- support and resistance
- trend quality
- volume behavior
- relative strength vs peers
- whether the setup is actionable or only worth watching

Why:
The platform should assist research, not replace analyst judgment.

### Step 4: Prioritize by use case
I would group platform output into:
- trade-ready
- watchlist
- needs more validation
- weak / false positive

Why:
Not every strong-looking name should be treated equally.

---

## Mid-Session Research Workflow

### 1. Deepen analysis on top names
For 1 to 3 top-ranked names, I would add:
- sector context
- news check
- fundamental quick view
- recent earnings context
- risk factors

### 2. Convert shortlisted names into notes
For promising names, I would create a short research note including:
- stock summary
- why it appeared
- technical view
- sector support
- next validation points
- risk summary

### 3. Compare platform output with manual tools
I would benchmark platform output using:
- TradingView
- Chartink
- manual chart review
- financial metrics if needed

Why:
This helps assess whether the platform is producing trustworthy research support.

---

## End-of-Day Workflow

### 1. Review what worked
I would review:
- which top-ranked names stayed strong
- which names failed
- whether sector rankings remained valid
- whether trade-ready names were actually actionable

### 2. Capture benchmark notes
For each important name, I would note:
- platform verdict
- manual validation verdict
- final judgment
- whether the tool was early, accurate, noisy, or incomplete

### 3. Capture product feedback
At the end of the session, I would document:
- what was useful
- what was unclear
- what was missing
- what improved or reduced workflow speed

This is especially important in a product-testing role.

---

## Weekly Workflow

At the end of the week, I would review:

- which sectors were repeatedly strong
- whether the dashboard helped identify leadership early
- how often top-ranked names were valid
- common false positives
- which features helped most
- where the platform still needs better validation support

This would help create structured feedback for the founding, product, or engineering teams.

---

## How I Would Use This as a Power User

As a power user, I would not only use the platform to find stocks.

I would also evaluate:

- relevance of ranking logic
- trustworthiness of signals
- clarity of the interface
- research usefulness
- missing analyst workflows
- benchmark quality of generated output

That is how I would contribute beyond just using the tool.

---

## How This Workflow Supports the Role

This workflow matches the responsibilities of a Stock Market Research Analyst because it includes:

- daily use of the platform
- stock research using technical analysis
- addition of manual fundamental validation where needed
- structured product feedback
- testing platform quality in real conditions
- building benchmark-quality analyst notes

---

## What I Would Improve Next

To make this workflow stronger, I would next want the platform to support:

- factor-level explainability for each ranked stock
- stronger fundamental quick-view cards
- better benchmark tagging
- analyst note export
- better confidence scoring
- clearer false positive tracking

---

## Final View

A useful stock research platform should fit naturally into a real analyst workflow.

The most important question is not only:
“Can this tool generate output?”

It is:
“Can this tool help an analyst produce better, faster, and more reliable research every day?”

That is the standard I would use while working with such a platform.
